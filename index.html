FAQ

Clear answers for buyers.

Is this for personal finance or business finance?

Both. It covers household tracking while also including business-friendly tools like reports, ledgers, vendors, journal entries, and reconciliation.

What makes it different from a spreadsheet?

It gives users structured accounts, categorized transactions, budgets, recurring entries, and reports without manual spreadsheet maintenance.

Can it support professional-looking financial snapshots?

Yes. The app includes Profit & Loss and Balance Sheet reporting for understanding income, expenses, assets, liabilities, equity, and net worth.

Can I import data from my bank or a spreadsheet?

Yes. Prism supports CSV and Excel (.xlsx) import with automatic column detection. You can also enter transactions manually or set up recurring entries.

Where is my data stored? Is it in the cloud?

Your data is stored entirely on your local machine in a SQLite database. There is no cloud sync, no online account, and no telemetry. The app works completely offline.

Does Prism use double-entry accounting?

Yes. Prism is built on double-entry bookkeeping with a full chart of accounts (assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses), journal entries with sequential numbering, and balance verification on every post.

Can I export my data or print reports?

Yes. You can export transactions to CSV. Reports (Profit & Loss and Balance Sheet) have dedicated print views with clean, printer-friendly layouts.

How does reconciliation work?

Start a new reconciliation for any account, mark cleared transactions as they appear on your statement, then finish. Prism tracks reconciliation history so you can review past statement periods.
